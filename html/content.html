Documentation

This app allows users to see where source water protection activities may achieve different types and levels of co-benefits for people and nature. Users can also select individual watersheds and view more detailed information about what co-benefits may be enhanced by investments in that watershed.

Data layers in the Benefits Explorer are derivatives of the source data. Please note that the accuracy of each data layer is limited by the coverage and comprehensiveness of the original dataset. Please refer to the original data source for more detailed information, and to this site's data documentation for full methodology and citations.

Climate Change Mitigation/Carbon

Activities like reforestation, forest protection, and agricultural BMPs can mitigate climate change by avoiding carbon dioxide emissions through actions that safeguard carbon stored in soil and plants and sequestering carbon through plant growth. The following data layers provide information on watersheds where these activities have the greatest potential to contribute to climate change mitigation:

Standing Carbon

Total carbon stored in living, above-ground biomass summarized by HydroBASINS across the tropics. (Source data: Baccini et al. in review; Zarin et al., 2016)

Forest Loss

Cumulative area of forest loss between 2001 and 2014. (Source data: Hansen et al., 2013)

Reforestation Potential

Area of degraded forest lands that have the potential to be restored and reforested.  (Source data: WRI 2014)

Biodiversity

Strategic land management and protection can conserve habitat for terrestrial species and benefit aquatic species by improving water quality and flow reliability. The following data layers provide information on where biodiversity is threatened and where improvements in watershed health may benefit biodiversity conservation:

Freshwater Biodiversity Threat Index

Levels of threat to freshwater biodiversity within HydroBASINS normalized and standardized between zero and one. Some basins do not have average threat values due to insufficient coverage of the index data. (Source data: Vörösmarty et al., 2010)

Imperiled Terrestrial Species

Number of imperiled mammals, birds and amphibians whose status could be improved through source water protection activities. (Source data: BirdLife International and NatureServe 2015; IUCN 2016)

Human Health & Well Being

Communities not only benefit from improved water security but also from improved watershed health overall. The following data layers show where land protection could maintain pollination services providing valuable micronutrient and crop yield benefits, and also identify places with the greatest need for reducing excess nitrogen in surface waters:

Drop in Vitamin A Production w/o Pollination

The estimated percent reduction in vitamin A production associated with reduced pollination services. Data are summarized in HydroBASINS currently experiencing moderate or severe vitamin A deficiency, and basins with no data are of low concern for vitamin A deficiency. (Source data: Klein et al., 2007; Monfreda et al., 2008; WHO 2009; FAO 2016)

Agricultural Economic Value Lost w/o Pollination

The estimated percent of total agricultural economic value lost with reduced pollination services. (Source data: Monfreda et al., 2008; FAO 2016)

Total Annual Excess Nitrogen Application

Estimated total potential excess nitrogen loading per year into adjacent waterbodies. (Source data: EarthStat total fertilizer balance data; West et al., 2014)

Additional Data

Freshwater Rarity-weighted Richness

Freshwater ecoregions summarized by rarity-weighted richness (calculated as a combination of number of species and relative rarity of those species).  Highest biodiversity values are in the first quartile and lowest are in the fourth.  (Source data: Abell et al. 2011)

Terrestrial Rarity- weighted Richness

Terrestrial ecoregions summarized by rarity-weighted richness (calculated as a combination of number of species and relative rarity of those species).  Highest biodiversity values are in the first quartile and lowest are in the fourth.  (Source data: Abell et al. 2011)

Avoided Regional Extinctions (Number of Species)

Number of projected avoided regional species extinctions by terrestrial ecoregion. Predictions are based on species-area relationship models and changes in land use through reforestation and restoration opportunities. (Source data: WRI 2014; Hoskins et al. 2016; Chaudhary et al. 2015; BirdLife International and NatureServe 2015; IUCN 2016)

Proportion of unprotected natural land cover within source watersheds needed to meet Aichi Biodiversity Target 11

Countries that could meet Aichi Biodiversity Target 11 (at least 17% of land and inland waters protected) if implementing land protection as a source water protection activity outside existing protected areas. The different levels indicate the percentage of unprotected natural land cover within urban source watersheds, aggregated by country, that would need protection to meet the goal. (Source data: IUCN and UNEP-WCMC 2016)
